# The Paper Wall
## An Investigation into SALN Access and the Architecture of Transparency in the Philippines

**Published by [The Anglophone Blind Spot](https://ivanapark1.substack.com)**
**Author: Ivana Park**
**Date: June 2026**

---

## What This Investigation Documents

This repository contains the full investigative record of a journalist's attempt to access the Statement of Assets, Liabilities and Net Worth (SALN) — a mandatory public financial disclosure — of two provincial officials of Pampanga, Philippines, through the Office of the Ombudsman.

The investigation ran from 4 May to 4 June 2026. It produced thirteen documentary exhibits, a constitutional legal argument, a comparative experiment, and a formal response from the Director of the Public Assistance and Corruption Prevention Bureau of the Office of the Deputy Ombudsman for Luzon.

The SALNs were not obtained.

---

## Key Findings

1. The Office of the Ombudsman referred requests without substantive response on multiple occasions before instructing that the SALN Request Form must be submitted **in person** at their Quezon City office — a requirement with no basis in the text of applicable law.

2. When asked for the statutory authority behind the physical attendance requirement, the Ombudsman's office cited **Section 6 of Memorandum Circular No. 3, Series of 2025** — the section that explicitly states all requests shall be granted. The grant provision was cited as authority for denial.

3. A comparative request filed under a Filipino name with no journalism identification received the **identical physical attendance barrier in one day**, confirming the obstruction is structural, not targeted at foreign journalists.

4. The Ombudsman's office **spontaneously designated the Filipino requester as a journalist** despite no journalism claim in the request, then imposed press accreditation requirements on that basis. Asking for a public document is sufficient to be classified as press.

5. The government's FOI portal (foi.gov.ph) does not list the Office of the Ombudsman, the Civil Service Commission, or the Provincial Government of Pampanga as registered agencies. The portal's verification link initially returns an error. Registration was completed under a non-Filipino name using a Singapore driving licence — the citizenship restriction in the terms of service is not enforced.

6. On 26 May 2026, Director Raquel Rosario M. Cunanan-Marayag responded by formal letter (OFA-L-26-0726-M), offering a partial concession: video conference oath for overseas requesters, digital delivery confirmed possible. However, **payment must be made in cash, in person, at one of seven physical offices** across the Philippines. No remote payment channels exist.

7. Assistant Ombudsman Mico Clavano announced in October 2025 that MC No. 3 would ensure **"perpetual public access"** to digitised SALNs. His Director's letter requires cash at a window. These statements cannot both be true.

8. When asked to confirm in writing whether the absence of remote payment infrastructure means the right of access cannot be exercised by anyone outside physical reach of a cashier office, the Ombudsman's office responded — nine days later — with an acknowledgment of receipt.

## Legal Framework

The investigation engages the following instruments:

- **Republic Act No. 6713** — Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees. Establishes the SALN as a public document.
- **Executive Order No. 2 (s. 2016)** — Freedom of Information in the Executive Branch. Establishes a legal presumption in favour of access.
- **Memorandum Circular No. 3, Series of 2025** — Office of the Ombudsman. Section 6 states all requests shall be granted. Section 4 imposes physical attendance as procedure. The investigation documents how the office cited Section 6 as authority for denial.
- **Article III, Section 7, 1987 Philippine Constitution** — guarantees the right of access to information on matters of public concern.
- **OMB Form No. 9, Series of 2025** — SALN Request Form. Sets fee at ₱20 per page payable in cash only.

## The Comparative Experiment

A parallel SALN request was filed on 19 May 2026 under a Filipino name with no journalism identification, for the SALNs of a former governor of Pampanga (2007–2010).

Results:
- Response time: **one day** (vs two weeks for the primary request)
- Barrier imposed: **identical** physical attendance requirement
- Additional finding: the requester was **spontaneously designated as a journalist** by the Ombudsman's office despite no journalism claim, triggering press accreditation requirements
- The office cited **Section 3(a)** of MC No. 3 to the Filipino requester vs **Section 6** to this publication — inconsistent application of the same circular

## Methodology Note

This investigation uses only publicly available information and documentary evidence generated in the course of the journalist's own correspondence with the Ombudsman's office. No confidential sources were used. All exhibits are primary documents. The comparative experiment used a pseudonymous identity for the limited purpose of testing whether the obstruction was targeted or structural — a standard investigative methodology for documenting systemic discrimination in public services.
